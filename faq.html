Lessons & format

Standard lessons run 60 minutes. For younger students or intensive prep work, we can adjust to 45 or 90 minutes depending on what serves the learning best — and we'll discuss this with you upfront.

We use Zoom or Google Meet by default — whichever you're more comfortable with. Both work on any device with a camera and microphone. We'll send you the meeting link before each session.

For materials, homework, and notes, we typically use shared Google Docs and WhatsApp.

Yes — 100% of our lessons are individual. The only exception is our optional Online Speaking Club, which is intentionally group-based to give learners a community of peers to practice with. Even then, group sizes are kept small.

Most students see steady progress with 2–3 lessons per week. Exam-prep students often go intensive (4–5 per week), while semester-long learners may settle into 1–2. Your tutor will recommend a cadence based on your goals and timeline — but the choice is always yours.

Life happens. We ask for at least 12 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling, after which we can move your lesson to another available slot at no extra cost. Late cancellations may count toward your monthly lesson allowance.

Our methodology

We use a communicative approach grounded in solid grammar fundamentals. That means: you'll be speaking from lesson one, but you'll also understand why the language works the way it does. We avoid both rote memorization and chaotic conversation-only methods.

Our methods are calibrated per student — what works for an IELTS candidate differs from what works for a 12-year-old learning Spanish for travel.

Three ways:

  • Regular consultations where we review what's working and what isn't
  • Mock exams and assessments for exam-prep students
  • Skill checkpoints at key milestones (CEFR levels, semester ends)

You'll always know where you stand — no vague reassurances.
